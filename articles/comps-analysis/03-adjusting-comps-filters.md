## Help Content

**Title:** Adjusting Comps Filters
**Format:** Help article
**Audience:** New and power users
**Slug:** adjusting-comps-filters
**Purpose:** Refine comp results by adjusting date range, distance, property type, and other filters.

**Content:**

After an initial comp run, the results may need fine-tuning. Filters let you narrow or expand the comparable set to find the most relevant comps for your analysis.

### Available Filters

**Sale date range**
Controls which sales are included. The default is typically the last 6 months.

- Narrow to 3 months for active markets with frequent sales.
- Expand to 12 months for slow markets or unique properties.
- Shorter ranges yield more relevant data. Longer ranges capture more comps but may include outdated pricing.

**Distance radius**
Controls how far from the subject property comps can be.

- Default is typically 0.5 miles (urban) or 2 miles (suburban).
- Tighten to 0.25 miles for dense urban areas with many sales.
- Expand to 1 mile or more for rural areas with limited transactions.

**Property type**
Filter to a specific property type. Use this when your subject property type differs from neighboring properties.

- Single Family
- Condo
- Multi-Family (2-4 units)
- Townhome

**Beds and baths**
Set minimum and maximum bedroom and bathroom counts.

- Useful when the initial results include properties with very different layouts.
- Setting a range too tight may eliminate useful comps. Start wide and narrow gradually.

**Square footage**
Set minimum and maximum living area.

- Standard filter. Properties within 20% of your subject's square footage are most comparable.
- Loosen this filter in areas with diverse housing stock.

### How Filters Update Results

All filters update results live. As you adjust a slider or change a dropdown, the comps table refreshes to show only matching properties.

- Adding a filter removes comps that do not meet the new criteria.
- Removing a filter adds back comps that were previously excluded.
- The total comp count updates to show how many comparables match all active filters.

### Balancing Precision and Quantity

Aim for 4 to 8 high-quality comps. Too few (under 3) and your analysis lacks statistical weight. Too many (over 12) and you may be including properties that are not truly comparable.

- **Too few results** — Widen the date range, increase the distance radius, or remove optional filters.
- **Too many irrelevant results** — Tighten the distance, narrow the date range, or apply the property type filter.

### Saving Filtered Results

When you are satisfied with the filtered set:

- Click "Save to Property" to keep the current filtered comps attached to the property.
- The saved set preserves your filter selections. Future views of the comps use the same filters.
- You can re-filter later without losing the original comp run.
